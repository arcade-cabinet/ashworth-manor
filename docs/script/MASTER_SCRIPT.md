This file now mirrors the canonical narrative spine rather than maintaining an
independent alternate script.

Use these files as the authoritative story surfaces:

This script directory should remain the authoring-oriented mirror of that
canonical set, not a competing continuity.

---

## Current Canon

- The playable present is near-post-Victorian rather than modern.
- The player returns as lawful heir after the final caretaker's death.
- The opening begins on a solicitor packet page, then resolves at the front
  gate and valise.
- The house remains dark until the player earns warmth and light.
- The old `Captive / Mourning / Sovereign` weave model is no longer canonical.
- Ashworth now has three authored Elizabeth routes:
  - `Adult`
  - `Elder`
  - `Child`

---

## First Three Completions

1. `Adult`
2. `Elder`
3. `Child`

Later repeat runs may randomize among unlocked routes, but the first three
completions are fixed and authored.

---

## Shared Script Spine

1. Solicitor packet
   - legal inheritance, professional tone, no supernatural theatrics
2. Front gate arrival
   - hired cab departs, valise under sign, no one receives the player
3. Ceremonial approach
   - long drive, dark house, side routes promised but initially withheld
4. First house occupation
   - player unlocks the front door and makes the first warmth/light
5. Early ground-floor cluster
   - parlor, kitchen, foyer, and dining establish the house as lived family
     space
6. Elizabeth's first seizure
   - laugh, service opening, forced descent
7. Service basement reclamation
   - relight under pressure, restore gas, house-state changes
8. Midgame possession
   - walking-stick investigation phase, lit-house confidence
9. Route-specific late rupture
   - attic truth, crypt truth, or hidden-room truth
10. Music box resolution
   - every route resolves through Elizabeth's treasured object

---

## Route Distinctions

### Adult

- final truth in the attic
- clue bias toward letters, portraiture, and unfinished adult life

### Elder

- final truth in the crypt
- clue bias toward burial, endurance, and continuity that should have ended

### Child

- final truth in a sealed hidden room revealed through attic clues
- clue bias toward childhood memory, blocked architecture, and family erasure

---

## Authoring Rules

- Outside paperwork and estate signage stay neutral.
- Elizabeth's laugh only marks meaningful events.
- Major equipment phases replace prior ones rather than accumulating forever.
- The music box is not optional flavor. It is the invariant solve object.
