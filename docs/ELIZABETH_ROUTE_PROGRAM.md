# Elizabeth Route Program

This document defines Ashworth Manor's macro narrative structure.

It supersedes the older `Captive / Mourning / Sovereign` weave as the canonical
story model.

---

## Core Decision

Ashworth Manor should ship three bespoke authored Elizabeth storylines rather
than a PRNG macro-thread system.

Those storylines are:

1. `Adult`
2. `Elder`
3. `Child`

The first three completions are fixed in that order. Only later repeat runs may
randomize among unlocked routes.

---

## Why This Replaces the Old Weave

The old weave framing produced interesting tone variants, but it blurred the
core dramatic question. The new route program is stronger because it:

- anchors the whole game in Elizabeth's life truth
- makes each playthrough structurally distinct rather than cosmetically
  different
- lets geography, clue emphasis, and endings diverge with purpose
- turns replay into discovery of deeper strata rather than rerolling a mood seed

---

## Shared Spine vs Route Divergence

### Shared Spine

All three routes share:

- solicitor packet opening
- front gate and valise arrival
- ceremonial drive to the dark house
- first occupation and first warmth/light problem
- early ground-floor cluster
- Elizabeth's laugh and forced service descent
- service basement gas restoration
- stable midgame house light
- walking-stick investigation phase

### Divergence Rule

Routes should diverge in late game through:

- which spaces become most charged
- what kind of clues dominate
- how Elizabeth appears to the player
- where the music box is finally found

Routes should not feel like arbitrary reskins of one seed.

---

## Adult Route

### Signature

- forbidden biography
- lost adulthood
- a woman partially erased from the family record but not from the house

### Dominant Spaces

- parlor
- library
- attic

### Dominant Clue Types

- letters
- portraits
- adult private effects
- evidence of a life extended beyond the family's official story

### Endgame

- the attic is the final truth space
- the music box is found in the attic
- the player's childhood winding key and the attic truth finally meet

### Emotional Payoff

- Elizabeth did not remain only a child in memory
- she lived into adulthood and was still denied a full human life

---

## Elder Route

### Signature

- buried continuity
- a life that persisted far too long
- the family dead, but Elizabeth still not released

### Dominant Spaces

- great hall
- wine cellar
- crypt

### Dominant Clue Types

- burial records
- caretaker residue
- family omissions
- objects preserved past their rightful span

### Endgame

- the crypt is the final truth space
- the music box is found with Elder Elizabeth in the crypt

### Emotional Payoff

- the player discovers that Elizabeth's life did not end when the family said
  it did
- the burial world becomes personal rather than abstract funerary scenery

---

## Child Route

### Signature

- recovered childhood horror
- blocked architecture
- the deepest lie hidden inside the estate body itself

### Dominant Spaces

- attic
- childhood memory intrusions
- sealed hidden room

### Dominant Clue Types

- nursery traces
- sealed walls
- plastered-over or boarded-over architecture
- memory fragments from the player's own childhood visits

### Endgame

- the route initially resembles the attic-directed truth of the adult route
- attic clues expose the hidden room
- the music box is found in the sealed room, not merely in the attic

### Emotional Payoff

- the player reaches the foundational family crime: a child hidden, denied, and
  architecturally erased

---

## Music Box Rule

The music box is the invariant final solve object across all routes.

What changes is:

- who Elizabeth truly was in that playthrough
- where the box is found
- what meaning the box carries when it is finally played

This gives the game one constant symbolic center without flattening the routes
into sameness.

---

## Transition Rules

- Outside paperwork and estate signage remain neutral.
- The first real route coloration should come from the house, not the packet.
- Elizabeth's laugh only marks significant events.
- Major held-tool phases replace earlier ones:
  - `firebrand`
  - `walking stick`
  - `lantern hook`
