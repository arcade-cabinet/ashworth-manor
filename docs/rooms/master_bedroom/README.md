## Narrative Purpose

Lord Ashworth's guilt made manifest. The diary on the nightstand is the **CRITICAL CLUE** that reveals where the attic key is hidden. The jewelry box (locked — key in family crypt) contains Elizabeth's locket with a lock of hair — a counter-ritual component.

The master bedroom establishes:
- **Father's guilt** — diary confessing he hears Elizabeth crying through walls
- **The key location** — "The attic key is hidden in the library globe"
- **Violated intimacy** — unmade bed, one side. Lady Ashworth's side pristine.
- **The locket** — Lady Ashworth kept Elizabeth's baby portrait close
