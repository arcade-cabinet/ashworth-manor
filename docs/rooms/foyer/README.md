## Narrative Purpose

The grand entrance hall. Two stories high, grand staircase, chandelier. This is where the Ashworth family made their first impression — wealth, taste, authority. In the playable present it should also feel like a place the player half-remembers: not welcoming, not wholly strange, but familiar in the wrong proportions.

The foyer establishes:
- **The frozen moment** — clock stopped at 3:33, no ticking
- **The patriarch** — Lord Ashworth's portrait, "Rites of Passage" book
- **The first wrong reflection** — mirror that moves independently
- **The dark-house rule** — the hall is not pre-lit for the player
- **Failed infrastructure** — the wall plate is present, but the gas does not answer
- **Hub access** — connects to all ground floor rooms + stairs up
- **Return memory** — crossing into the hall should feel like stepping into a place once known at a child's height

**Visual:** Red Victorian wallpaper (wall_0, #732F26). Black and white marble checkered floor (floor0). Ornate plaster ceiling (cieling0). Grand staircase with carved banisters. Chandelier dominates the center, but on the standard route it hangs dead above a hall lit only by moon spill and memory. The floor dressing now resolves into a stronger central runner toward the stair/parlor axis, while paired south-side consoles and lamps read as expensive fixtures that have gone cold.

**Audio:** Conspicuous ABSENCE of clock ticking. Distant settling sounds. Marble footstep echo. Chandelier crystal tinkling (very faint).

**Tone:** Imposing, cold authority. Beautiful but wrong. Crossing in should feel like entering a house that still expects proper conduct even though it can no longer perform ordinary hospitality.
