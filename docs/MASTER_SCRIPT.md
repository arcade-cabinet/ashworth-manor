# Master Script — Canonical Spine

This document is the current narrative spine for Ashworth Manor. It replaces
older room-by-room script assumptions that were built around the legacy
`Captive / Mourning / Sovereign` weave model.

The current canon is:

- neutral solicitor packet and estate signage
- three authored Elizabeth routes
- shared early/mid spine
- bespoke late-game divergence
- music box as the invariant final solve object

## Shared Stages

### Stage 0 — Solicitor Packet

Narrative job:

- justify the return
- establish legal possession
- begin with external, professional reality rather than ghost-story theatrics

Canonical beats:

- open on an inner inheritance page from a solicitor's packet
- the estate has already passed to the player
- the packet is precise, neutral, and socially proper

### Stage 1 — Front Gate Arrival

Narrative job:

- convert legal premise into bodily arrival
- establish burden, wealth, and etiquette failure

Canonical beats:

- first-person resolves as the hired cab departs
- the valise sits beneath the estate sign where the driver left it
- no one is present to receive the player
- the player opens the valise and establishes inventory
- the estate sign remains neutral and respectable

### Stage 2 — Ceremonial Approach

Narrative job:

- make the player commit to Ashworth as a place of distance and controlled
  wealth

Canonical beats:

- front gate, drive lower, drive upper, and front steps form one opening chain
- the mansion remains mostly withheld on first approach
- side routes are only revealed at the forecourt and are initially gated
- the player unlocks the front door personally

### Stage 3 — First Occupation of the House

Narrative job:

- make the house feel dark, cold, and not ready to receive anyone
- let the first real answer come from a family-centered interior space

Canonical beats:

- the mansion is dark on arrival
- the player must make the first warmth and light
- the earliest major house answer belongs to the first family-centered room
  logic of the active route, not to outside props or documents

### Stage 4 — Early Ground-Floor Cluster

Narrative job:

- teach parlor, kitchen, foyer, and dining relations as lived spaces rather
  than puzzle boxes
- let the player feel temporarily in control

Canonical beats:

- early ground-floor exploration should breathe
- nothing human is encountered
- environment remains alive, house remains socially dead

### Stage 5 — Elizabeth's First Seizure

Narrative job:

- make Elizabeth's agency undeniable
- force the player below through service architecture

Canonical beats:

- after meaningful early exploration, the player inspects a kitchen-side
  service opening
- Elizabeth's laugh signals the event
- the player is forced down into the service basement
- the fall is consequential but not meant to kill

### Stage 6 — Service Basement and Gas Restoration

Narrative job:

- reveal how the estate functioned out of sight of the residents
- move the player from improvised light to restored house-state light

Canonical beats:

- the player relights themselves under pressure
- the service basement reads as a real maintenance world, not monster basement
- restoring gas changes the house state and stabilizes light
- the player re-emerges via shared/utilitarian circulation

### Stage 7 — Midgame Possession

Narrative job:

- shift from survival to deliberate investigation
- let the player reclaim the estate room by room in social and mechanical
  terms

Canonical beats:

- walking stick becomes the active held investigative state
- stable house light is now normal
- route-specific clue pressure begins shaping what feels charged
- grounds, service routes, and private rooms become legible as one estate

### Stage 8 — Late-Game Transition

Narrative job:

- strip away the stability the player has earned
- force the route-specific truth to the surface

Canonical beats:

- a major transition removes the walking-stick phase
- late darkness, descent, attic rupture, or equivalent route-specific break
  takes over
- the player enters the final Elizabeth truth architecture

## Route Summaries

### Adult Route

Signature:

- stolen adulthood
- a biography interrupted but not fully extinguished

Late structure:

- late descent yields the lantern-on-hook phase
- basement and burial-side tooling eventually open the attic as the final truth
- the music box is found in the attic

Ending meaning:

- the player confronts the fact that Elizabeth lived beyond the family story
  and was denied a full adult life

### Elder Route

Signature:

- continuity that should have ended
- burial and memory turned accusatory

Late structure:

- the attic becomes the route's late rupture point
- clues and access changes drive the player toward the wine cellar and crypt
- the music box is found with Elder Elizabeth in the crypt

Ending meaning:

- the player discovers a life that lasted far longer than the family ever
  admitted and still was never allowed peace

### Child Route

Signature:

- sealed childhood horror
- the deepest wound hidden inside architecture

Late structure:

- it initially resembles the adult route's attic-driven truth
- attic clues expose the sealed hidden room
- the music box is found in the hidden room rather than the attic proper

Ending meaning:

- the player reaches the foundational family crime: a child erased and
  plastered over inside the estate itself

## Script Rules

- The music box always resolves the route.
- Elizabeth's laugh only marks major event transitions.
- Each major held-tool phase consumes the previous one.
- Outside legal or estate-facing documents remain neutral.
- The first three routes are authored and ordered, not random.
